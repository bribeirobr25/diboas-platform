## Core Insight
> People share knowledge they've gained, not sales pitches they've received.

# PART 8: WHAT NOT TO DO

## Avoid These Framings

| ‚ùå Don't Say | ‚úÖ Instead Say |
|-------------|---------------|
| "I earned ‚Ç¨X while sleeping" | "Here's something I learned about money" |
| "Banks are stealing from us" | "Here's what banks don't explain" |
| "Join the movement" | "You might find this interesting" |
| "I'm #847 on the waitlist" | "I'm learning something new about money" |
| "Get in early" | "Worth understanding" |
| "They lied to us" | "There's something they didn't tell us" |
| "She died with nothing" | "It still wasn't enough" |
| "I grew up poor" | "I'm from Rio" |

## Why This Matters

- Educational framing builds trust
- Learning is shareable; bragging isn't (for most people)
- Curiosity spreads better than outrage
- Bar's voice is teacher, not warrior
