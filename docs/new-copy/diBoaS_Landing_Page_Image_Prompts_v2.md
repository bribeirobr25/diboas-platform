## Competitor Visual Audit Summary

Sites analyzed: Nubank, N26, Wise, Monzo, Inter, Rocket Money, Revolut, C6 Bank, Qonto, Chime

### What the Best Fintech Landing Pages Actually Use

| Visual Type | Usage | Who Does It Best |
|---|---|---|
| **Phone mockups showing app UI** | Every single competitor. THE most important visual. | All — this is table stakes |
| **Real lifestyle photography + phone overlay** | Dominant pattern for feature sections | Rocket Money, N26, Monzo |
| **Physical card product shots** | Very common for payment/card sections | N26, Monzo, Wise |
| **Hand-holding-phone close-ups** | Standard hero and feature imagery | N26, Monzo, Wise |
| **Real people in real situations** | Used for emotional/trust sections | Rocket Money, Nubank, Inter |
| **Simple playful illustrations** | Used sparingly alongside photography | Monzo (colorful icons), Wise (security lock) |
| **Trust badges** | App Store/Google Play ratings, press logos, Trustpilot | Wise, Rocket Money, Monzo |
| **Minimal 3D abstract art** | Very sparingly — accent only | Nubank (purple cube), Wise (globe) |

### What They Do NOT Use

- Full-illustration-only pages (zero competitors do this)
- Abstract technology illustrations
- AI-generated "concept art" style images
- Overly futuristic or sci-fi aesthetics
- Heavy gradient overlays on every section
- Complex infographic illustrations

### Key Insight

**The formula is: Real Photography + Phone Mockup + One Signature Brand Illustration Style.**

Every successful fintech uses real photography as its backbone, phone mockups as its proof, and one distinctive illustration style (Monzo's colorful icons, Wise's 3D objects, N26's teal accents) as its signature seasoning.

### Updated Visual Strategy

| Layer | What | How |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary (60%)** | Real lifestyle photography | Warm, diverse, everyday moments — NOT stock photography feeling |
| **Secondary (30%)** | Phone & card mockups | Clean device frames showing diBoaS app UI |
| **Accent (10%)** | Simple flat illustrations | diBoaS signature style — minimal, teal/gold, geometric |

### Photography Direction

- **Lighting:** Natural, warm. Golden hour or soft indoor light. Never harsh fluorescent.
- **People:** Diverse always. Real expressions, not posed smiles. Candid moments.
- **Settings:** Everyday life — coffee shops, kitchens, parks, walking, transit. Never offices or boardrooms.
- **Color treatment:** Slightly warm tone, with teal accents introduced through clothing, objects, or post-production tint.
- **Phones in shots:** When visible, always show a clean, modern smartphone (no specific brand). Screen should glow teal.

### Illustration Direction (Accent Use Only)

- **Style:** Flat vector with soft rounded corners. Think Monzo's playful icons but in diBoaS teal/navy/gold palette.
- **Use for:** Icons, badges, stat callouts, decorative elements alongside photography.
- **Never:** Full-section illustrations replacing photography.

## Section 1: Hero

**Asset needed:** Hero image or composition (desktop: 1440×800px, mobile: 390×600px)
**Placement:** Right side or behind headline with overlay
**Reference:** N26 (person holding phone), Monzo (hand with card), Rocket Money (phone with app)

### Prompt — Option A: Hand Holding Phone (Recommended)

```
Close-up photograph of a young person's hand holding a modern smartphone. The phone screen glows with a clean teal (#00D4AA) fintech app interface showing a balance and action buttons. The background is softly blurred, suggesting an outdoor café or park setting with warm golden hour light. The person wears a casual sleeve (not a suit). A second hand rests on a wooden table nearby. Warm skin tones, natural light, shallow depth of field. The mood is calm confidence — someone who just took control of their finances. Shot on 35mm, editorial quality, no text, no logos, no watermarks. 16:9 aspect ratio.
```

### Prompt — Option B: Person Walking + Phone

```
Wide-angle lifestyle photograph from slightly behind a young woman walking on a city sidewalk, holding her phone in one hand. The phone screen shows a teal-glowing financial app interface. She's dressed casually in a light jacket. The city around her is slightly blurred with warm morning light. The scene communicates everyday freedom — money that moves with you. Diverse, natural, candid. Shot from waist up. Warm tones with teal color accent on the phone screen. No text, no logos, 16:9 wide format.
```

### Prompt — Option C: Card + Phone Flat Lay

```
Top-down flat lay photograph of a modern smartphone lying on a light wood table next to a sleek teal (#00D4AA) debit card. The phone screen shows a clean fintech dashboard with teal accent elements. A coffee cup sits in the corner, slightly out of focus. Natural morning light from a window casts soft shadows. The composition is clean and minimal — just the essentials. Product photography style but warm and lived-in, not sterile. No text, no logos. 4:3 aspect ratio.
```

## Implementation Notes

### For Flux Pro Ultra (Available Now)

Flux excels at photorealistic outputs. Recommended for all lifestyle photography prompts (Sections 1-5, 7-8, 10). Use `flux-pro-ultra` model for best quality.

For card and phone mockups, generate the base image with Flux, then composite the actual diBoaS app UI in post-production (Figma/Photoshop). AI-generated "app screens" won't match your real UI.

### Post-Production Workflow

1. **Generate base images** with Flux Pro Ultra (4 variants per prompt, pick best)
2. **Color-correct** all images to match diBoaS palette (teal #00D4AA as accent)
3. **Composite phone screens** — replace AI-generated abstract UI with actual app screenshots
4. **Composite card designs** — replace abstract card with actual diBoaS card design from Figma
5. **Apply consistent color grade** across all images (warm + teal)
6. **Export** at required dimensions per section

## Locale Considerations

All images are designed to be **locale-agnostic** (no visible text, no location-specific landmarks, diverse subjects). The same images work across EN, PT-BR, DE, and ES landing pages.

For locale-specific flavor, rely on **copy and UI overlay text** rather than different photography per market. This is consistent with how N26, Wise, and Nubank handle multi-market visual assets.
