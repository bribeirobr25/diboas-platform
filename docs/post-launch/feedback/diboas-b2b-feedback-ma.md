# diBoaS for Business: A B2B User's First Impressions

After reviewing the diBoaS for Business landing page (www.diboas.com/en/business) as a business owner who might stumble upon it, here is my assessment and answers to your questions.

## What is diBoaS for Business?

diBoaS for Business positions itself as a financial solution designed to help businesses, particularly small to medium-sized ones and startups, combat common financial inefficiencies. It's not a traditional bank but rather a platform built on decentralized finance (DeFi) technology. The core value proposition is twofold: to eliminate the high fees associated with card payments and to provide a way for businesses to earn returns on their idle cash, which would otherwise be sitting in a bank account earning minimal interest. The messaging is direct and speaks to the pain points of business owners who feel they are losing money to an unfair system.

## What can be done with diBoaS for Business?

The platform offers a clear set of functionalities aimed at improving a company's financial health:

*   **Eliminate Card Processing Fees:** Businesses can accept card payments and, according to diBoaS, keep 100% of the revenue, bypassing the typical 2-3% fee charged by traditional payment processors. This is a significant and immediate cost-saving measure.
*   **Earn Returns on Idle Cash:** Instead of letting cash reserves sit in a bank account earning little to no interest, diBoaS allows businesses to invest this idle cash into various strategies to generate returns. They provide simulators to project potential earnings.
*   **Instant Global Payments:** The platform enables businesses to pay contractors, suppliers, and freelancers anywhere in the world instantly and without fees.
*   **Financial Insights:** Through their AI assistant, "Adelaide," diBoaS provides market intelligence and financial insights, acting as a virtual advisor for business owners.

## Why give the email to diBoaS for Business?

Similar to their consumer-facing page, providing an email address is for **joining the waitlist** to get early access to the platform. The page indicates they are starting with a limited number of "founding businesses" to ensure a high level of service. Giving your email is the way to secure a spot in this early cohort and be notified when the platform is ready to onboard new clients.

## Why use diBoaS for Business over other options?

diBoaS for Business is not trying to be a direct replacement for a company's primary bank account but rather a powerful supplementary tool. Its advantages over traditional options (like standard business bank accounts, payment processors like Stripe or Square, or even other fintechs) are quite specific:

| Feature/Aspect | diBoaS for Business | Traditional Options (Banks, Payment Processors) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Card Processing Fees** | 0% | 2-3% + additional fees |
| **Idle Cash Returns** | Offers access to DeFi investment strategies with potential for significant returns (e.g., historical average of 7%). | Very low to no interest on standard business checking accounts. |
| **International Payments** | Instant and free global payments. | Slow (several business days) and often involve high fees for international wires. |
| **Control and Transparency** | Self-custodial, with a clear breakdown of the (minimal) fees. | Custodial, with less transparency on how the institution uses your funds. Fees can be complex and hidden. |
| **Risk Profile** | Higher risk due to its DeFi nature and lack of government deposit insurance. This is explicitly stated. | Lower risk, with government deposit insurance (e.g., FDIC in the US) providing a safety net. |

In essence, a business would use diBoaS to **aggressively cut costs** on payment processing and to **actively generate revenue** from its cash reserves in a way that traditional banking does not allow. It is for businesses that are comfortable with a higher risk tolerance in exchange for potentially much higher financial efficiency and returns. The platform is a good fit for businesses that are digitally savvy and feel constrained by the fees and low returns of the conventional financial system.
