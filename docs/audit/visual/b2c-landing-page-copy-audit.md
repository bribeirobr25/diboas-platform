# B2C Landing Page Copy Audit

**Date:** February 23, 2026
**Audited URL:** `http://localhost:3000/en`
**Audited by:** Copywriter Skill (Direct Response Copy Operating System + Writing Engine)
**Scope:** All 11 sections of the B2C landing page (English locale)

---

## Executive Summary

The landing page has a strong structural foundation and genuine brand voice. The hero headline is a standout — contrarian, emotionally resonant, movement-grade. The real-life scenarios section is the best-written section on the page. The fee transparency table is a rare competitive advantage that most fintechs avoid.

However, from a direct response perspective, the page underperforms in 5 critical areas:

1. **Zero pain quantification** — the page never makes the reader feel the cost of inaction
2. **Every CTA is command-oriented** — no CTA describes a benefit
3. **No So-What chains** — features are listed but emotional/financial payoffs aren't developed
4. **No disqualification** — the page never says who diBoaS is NOT for (key trust signal)
5. **No honest limitations** — everything is positioned as perfect, which reduces believability

**Overall Claim Ladder Level:** Hypothesis (Level 4) — pre-launch, no user data, no track record yet. The copy should lean harder on mechanism + deliverables + process proof + honest limitations rather than implied outcomes.

### 1. HERO SECTION

**Current copy:**
- H1: `"The system isn't broken. It's working exactly as designed. Just not for you."`
- Sub: `"Money should move free and instant. Savings should grow. Your money should work while you sleep."`
- CTA: `"Try Demo"`

**What works:**
- H1 is excellent — contrarian, pattern interrupt, creates tension, identity-level language. This headline would make Eugene Schwartz nod. It targets Unaware → Problem-aware perfectly.
- The three-period structure in H1 creates a slippery slide: statement → twist → personal punch.

**What needs improvement:**

| Issue | Framework Violated | Fix |
|-------|-------------------|-----|
| Sub-headline crams 3 value props into 1 sentence with no specificity | Headline Master Formula: needs [specific outcome] + [timeframe or contrast] | Pick ONE value prop for the sub. E.g., "Send money anywhere, instantly, for free. Then make it grow while you sleep." — lead with the primary hook (free transfers) then tease the second |
| "Money should move free and instant" — "should" is passive/aspirational, not active | Voice markers: internet-native = direct, not aspirational | "Money moves free and instant. Here." or "Your money. Free to move. Everywhere. Now." |
| CTA "Try Demo" is command-oriented | CTA framework: describe benefit, not action | Replace with: **"See your money in action"** or **"Play with $100 (it's fake)"** or **"See how it works — no signup"** |
| No friction reducer below CTA | CTA framework: [Risk reversal] + [Social proof] + [Speed/ease] | Add below-CTA microcopy: "No signup. No real money. 30 seconds." |
| No specificity in sub-headline: how free? how instant? what growth? | Specificity principle: specific > vague always | Quantify at least one thing: "Send $500 to São Paulo in 4 seconds. Fee: $0.00." |

**Suggested rewrite (sub-headline + CTA):**
```
Sub: "Send money anywhere. Instantly. For free. Then make it grow while you sleep."

CTA button: "See your money in action"
Below CTA: "No signup. No real money. Takes 30 seconds."
```

### 7. WHAT'S THE CATCH?

**Current copy:**
```
"Fair question. Here's the honest answer:"
"There isn't one!"
"The best financial opportunities were locked behind big banks and hedge funds. You needed money to make money."

**What works:**
- Directly addresses the #1 objection — proactive objection handling.
- "We didn't invent this. We just made it simple enough" — honest, builds credibility.
- "We win when you win. When you grow, we grow." — alignment messaging.

**What needs improvement:**

| Issue | Framework Violated | Fix |
|-------|-------------------|-----|
| "There isn't one!" feels dismissive and slightly salesy | Honest limitations = trust marker. "No catch" is what every scam says | Replace with honest limitation: "There's no hidden catch. But here's what's real: your money is in DeFi protocols, not a bank vault. That means potentially higher returns — but also real risk. We show you both sides." |
| "Blockchain" mentioned explicitly — triggers crypto skepticism in unaware audience | Awareness escalation: lead with outcome, not mechanism | Remove "(Blockchain)" or replace: "New financial technology" or "open-source financial infrastructure" — don't name the tech |
| "You needed money to make money" is a cliché | Anti-AI detox: generic phrases | More specific: "You needed $10,000 just to get in the door. Then they charged you $25/month to keep it open." |
| No mechanism explanation — HOW are interests aligned? | Mechanism/reason-why framework | Explain the 0.39% fee model explicitly: "We charge 0.39% when you invest. That's it. If you put in $100, we make 39 cents. The only way we make more money is if you do." |
| Section ends without a CTA or forward-pull | Slippery slide: every section should pull to the next | Add a bucket brigade: "But don't take our word for it. Try it yourself." → links to demo section |

**Suggested rewrite:**
```
"Fair question. Here's the real answer:

We charge 0.39% when you invest. That's it.

Put in $100, we make 39 cents. The only way we earn more is if YOUR money grows.

No monthly fees. No hidden spreads. No withdrawal penalties.

How? Open-source financial infrastructure cut out the branches, the executives, and the legacy systems. We pass the savings to you.

Is there risk? Yes. Your money is in DeFi protocols, not a bank vault. Returns can fluctuate. We show you both sides — always.

## Cross-Cutting Issues (Apply to Entire Page)

### ISSUE 1: Zero Pain Quantification

**Violation:** The page never makes the reader feel the cost of doing nothing.

**Writing Engine principle:** "Makes vague problem concrete. Reader can calculate: 'Is this worth it?'"

**Current state:** The page talks about what diBoaS offers but never quantifies what the current system costs people.

**Fix:** Add pain math in at least 2 locations:

- **Hero or Origin Story:** "Americans pay $12 billion in bank fees every year. The average checking account costs $180/year in monthly charges alone."
- **Fee Table intro:** "The average person loses $300–$500/year to bank fees, transfer charges, and hidden spreads. Here's what that looks like — and what it looks like with us."
- **Real-Life Scenarios:** Add one cost comparison per card. "That $50 to Mom? Western Union: $9.99 + 3 days. diBoaS: $0 + instant."

### ISSUE 2: Every CTA is Command-Oriented

**Violation:** All CTAs describe the ACTION, not the BENEFIT.

**Writing Engine:** "Weak: Sign Up / Learn More / Buy Now. Strong: See the exact template I used / Start building / Enter your new home."

**Current CTAs on page:**
- "Try Demo" (hero) → ❌ command
- "Try Full Demo" (demo section) → ❌ command
- "Future You" (demo section) → ❓ unclear
- "Join the Waitlist" (social proof) → ❌ command
- "Join Waitlist" (waitlist section) → ❌ command

**Suggested replacements:**
| Current | Replacement |
|---------|-------------|
| "Try Demo" | "See your money in action" |
| "Try Full Demo" | "Play with $1,000 (it's fake)" |
| "Future You" | "See what your $100 becomes" |
| "Join the Waitlist" | "Get early access" |
| "Join Waitlist" | "Save my spot" |

### ISSUE 3: No Disqualification Anywhere

**Violation:** The page never says who diBoaS is NOT for.

**Writing Engine:** "Flips from 'please buy' to 'prove you're worthy.' Creates velvet rope effect. Pre-filters bad customers."

**Fix:** Add a disqualification element. Options:
- FAQ question: "Is diBoaS for everyone?" → "No." (see FAQ section above)
- Separate mini-section near the bottom, above FAQ:
  ```
  "diBoaS is for you if you want control over your money, transparency about costs,
  and access to opportunities that used to require $10,000 minimums.

  diBoaS is NOT for you if you want someone else to make decisions for you,
  or if you're uncomfortable with new technology."
  ```

---

### ISSUE 4: No Bucket Brigades or Transition Hooks

**Violation:** Sections are islands. Nothing pulls the reader from one section to the next.

**Writing Engine:** "Short phrases that smooth transitions: 'Here's why', 'But that's not even the best part', 'Now here's the thing...'"

**Fix:** Add a single transition line between key sections:
- After Hero → "Here's why I'm building this." (→ Origin Story)
- After Origin Story → "And here's how it works." (→ Product Carousel)
- After Real-Life Scenarios → "Now let's talk money." (→ Fee Table)
- After What's the Catch → "Don't take our word for it. Try it." (→ Demo)
- After Demo → "Still not sure?" (→ FAQ)

These can be small text elements between sections or the closing line of each section.

---

### ISSUE 5: Awareness Level Escalation is Bumpy

**Current flow:** Unaware (hero) → Story (origin) → Product-aware (carousel) → Problem-aware (scenarios) → Product-aware (features) → Product-aware (fees) → Objection handling (catch) → Most-aware (demo/waitlist)

**Problem:** The jump from Unaware (hero identity statement) to Product-aware (carousel showing features) is too fast. The reader hasn't had their problem agitated yet.

**Better flow:**
1. **Unaware → Problem-aware:** Hero + Origin Story (identity + problem naming) ✅ already works
2. **Problem-aware → Solution-aware:** Real-Life Scenarios should come BEFORE the Product Carousel — show the pain/desire first, then show the solution
3. **Solution-aware → Product-aware:** Product Carousel + Feature Carousel (mechanism + features)
4. **Product-aware → Most-aware:** Fees + Catch + Demo + FAQ + Waitlist

**Recommended section reorder:**
```
1. Hero (Unaware → Problem-aware)
2. Origin Story (Problem-aware — emotional anchor)
3. Real-Life Scenarios (Problem-aware → Solution-aware — "this is what's possible")
4. Product Carousel (Solution-aware — "here's how it works")
5. Feature Carousel (Solution → Product-aware)
6. Fees Table (Product-aware — comparison/proof)
7. What's the Catch (Objection handling)
8. Demo (Most-aware — trial)
9. Social Proof (Most-aware — validation)
10. FAQ (Most-aware — final objections)
11. Waitlist (Most-aware — action)
```

The current order has Real-Life Scenarios at position 4, after the Product Carousel. Moving scenarios to position 3 (before the carousel) creates a better awareness escalation: "here's the problem" → "here's what the solution looks like in real life" → "here's the product."

### ISSUE 7: "Blockchain" Appears Twice — Triggers Crypto Skepticism

**Locations:**
- Product Carousel slide 1: "stable cryptocurrencies (stablecoins)"
- What's the Catch: "New technology (Blockchain)"
- FAQ Q2: "modern technology (Blockchain)"

**Problem:** For Unaware and Problem-aware users (the primary target), "blockchain" and "cryptocurrency" can trigger immediate skepticism or dismissal. The brand strategy says lead with utility, not technology.

**Fix:**
- Replace "stablecoins" explanation: "Your money is stored as digital dollars — always worth exactly $1."
- Replace "Blockchain" mentions: "open-source financial infrastructure" or simply "new financial technology"
- Only use crypto-specific language in the investing/strategy sections where the user has already been warmed up.

---

### ISSUE 8: No Honest Limitations

**Violation:** The page presents everything as perfect. "There isn't one [catch]!" actually reduces believability.

**Writing Engine:** "Honesty about imperfection builds credibility. Shows you're not hiding anything. Makes other claims more believable."

**Fix:** Add 1-2 honest limitations:
- In "What's the Catch" section: "Is there risk? Yes. DeFi protocols aren't bank vaults. Yields fluctuate. We monitor 24/7 and battle-test against past crashes — but we can't guarantee returns. Nobody can."
- In FAQ: "What if something goes wrong?" answer (see FAQ section above)
- Consider adding a small "What we can't do (yet)" note: "We can't do wire transfers, we don't support every country yet, and our app is still invite-only. We're building."
