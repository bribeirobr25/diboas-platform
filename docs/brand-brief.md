# Brand Brief — diBoaS

## Product

- **Name:** diBoaS (named after founder's grandmother Adelaide from Rio de Janeiro)
- **Category:** Non-custodial OneFi (One Finance) platform — guided DeFi access layer
- **Platform:** Web-first (Q2 2026 launch), mobile later
- **What it actually does:**
  - Goal-based investment strategies (Safe Harbor, Beat Inflation, Long Game) starting from €5/$5/R$10
  - Stablecoin-powered P2P send/receive between diBoaS users (free, instant)
  - Buy/sell digital assets (BTC, ETH, SOL, tokenized gold, tokenized stocks)
  - Non-custodial wallets via Turnkey (MPC, zero diBoaS key shares)
  - Fiat on/off-ramp via Onramper (handles KYC)
  - Adelaide newsletter — standalone product, usable without a diBoaS wallet
- **What it explicitly does NOT do:**
  - Never holds fiat or crypto (non-custodial interface only)
  - No credit, lending, or BNPL — permanently excluded
  - No universal payment rail — P2P works only between diBoaS users
  - No financial advice — software provider/interface only
  - No deposit insurance — users own their funds directly (this is a feature, not a gap)

## Audience

- **Primary user:** People excluded by traditional banking — mainstream consumers who want their idle money to work harder but find existing financial tools intimidating, expensive, or inaccessible
- **Their context:** US, EU (Germany focus), and Brazil simultaneously. Four languages: EN, DE, ES, PT-BR
- **Their pain points:** Banks pay near-zero on savings, high fees for international transfers, $10,000 minimums exclude them, financial jargon creates paralysis, existing crypto/DeFi tools are intimidating
- **Their emotional state when they arrive:** Curious but skeptical. Tired of being told the system works for them when it doesn't. Want control but don't want complexity.
- **Their alternatives today:** Traditional bank savings (0.1-2% APY), Revolut, Nubank (Brazil), Coinbase, keeping cash idle
- **What they compare us to:** Revolut, Nubank, Robinhood — even though the comparison is wrong. We are a side pocket, not a replacement.

## Emotional target

This product should feel:
- Warm, honest, and human — like talking to a friend who knows finance, not a corporation
- Trustworthy and transparent — every fee visible, every risk stated plainly
- Empowering but not overwhelming — "you can do this" energy without drowning in options

This product should NOT feel:
- Like another crypto app or DeFi protocol
- Corporate, cold, or impersonal
- Like a tech-bro startup chasing the next trend
- Needy for attention or gamified for addiction
- Like it's trying to replace your bank (it's a side pocket, not a replacement)

## Positioning

- **What problem it solves:** Your idle money is losing value sitting in a bank. diBoaS puts it to work earning yield, growing into real assets, and moving across borders — without giving up ownership.
- **What makes it different:** Goal-based wizard UX + DeFi yield + non-custodial ownership + tokenized multi-asset access + cross-border P2P + no financial literacy required — across US, EU, and Brazil simultaneously. No competitor assembles this full stack.
- **The enemy:** Traditional banks. Not fintechs, not other crypto apps. Banks that pay people nothing on their savings and charge for the privilege.
- **Category clichés to avoid:** "Another crypto app." "Simplified DeFi." "Global PIX." "Better Coinbase." "Fintech disruptor." Any positioning that invites direct comparison to well-funded incumbents.
- **One sentence a user would say to recommend us:** "It's how I send money for free."
- **North star (12 months):** B2C retail user volume

## Visual references

## Brand notes

- **Tone of voice:** First person ("I" not "we") when Bar is speaking. Warm, direct, honest. Short sentences. Conversational, not corporate. The Adelaide story is the emotional foundation — every piece of consumer-facing language must pass the Adelaide Filter: "would grandmother understand this?"
- **Voice example:** "Your money is in your wallet. Not mine. If diBoaS shut down tomorrow, your money would still be there."
- **Motion style:** Subtle and purposeful. Transitions that feel natural, not robotic. Loading states that communicate what's happening. No decorative animation. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- **Icon / illustration style:** Custom SVG icon system organized by financial domain themes (banking/turquoise, crypto/coral, DeFi/purple, investing/purple, neutral/currentColor). 25 icons, 24x24 viewBox, stroke-width 2, `currentColor` for theming.
- **Mascot system:** Three mascots (Acqua/beginner/turquoise, Mystic/intermediate/purple, Coral/advanced/coral) — used for guidance, onboarding, and personality. Not used for navigation or core UI.
- **Visual metaphor:** "The door" — Bar built the door that Adelaide never had access to. The product is the door to financial tools that were previously locked behind minimums and jargon.
- **Human touch elements:** First-person voice from the founder. Adelaide story as emotional anchor. "I read every email" as direct line to founder. Hand-drawn or slightly imperfect accents welcome as anti-AI signals. Warm textures over flat surfaces.

## Product reality rules

- **Example content that feels believable:** Fee comparisons showing exact diBoaS fees vs. bank/competitor fees. Yield ranges (4-8% Safe Harbor, 6-12% Steady Growth) marked as historical, not guaranteed. Strategy examples with real asset classes. Cross-border send scenarios between US/EU/Brazil.
- **Features, pricing, and integrations that are CONFIRMED:**
  - Fee Lab v3.4: Add Money 0.48%, Cash Out 0.48%, Buy/Invest FREE, Sell/Close 0.39% ($0.25 min / $25 max), Send FREE, Swap FREE, Bridge FREE, Strategy Entry FREE, Strategy Exit 0.39%
  - Minimums: €5 (ES/DE), $5 (EN), R$10 (PT-BR)
  - Wallet: Turnkey MPC (zero diBoaS key shares)
  - On-ramp/KYC: Onramper
  - Whitelisted protocols: Sky, Aave, Compound, Jito, Sanctum, Jupiter JLP (Arbitrum + Solana)
- **Things the AI must NEVER invent as fact:**
  - Guaranteed returns or specific future yield percentages
  - Regulatory registration status (not registered with SEC/CFTC/FinCEN — this is a fact, not a claim)
  - "Send money to anyone" — P2P is between diBoaS users only
  - Deposit insurance or fund protection guarantees
  - Legacy fee figures: 0.75%, 0.12%, 0.09%, subscription tiers — these are OUTDATED
  - Smart contract audit status (not yet decided)
  - Specific launch dates beyond "Q2 2026"
- **Content that must be labeled "illustrative" if generated:** Strategy return projections, portfolio growth scenarios, balance examples, user testimonials
- **Regulatory or compliance sensitivity:**
  - EU/MiCA: Verbatim MiCA disclaimer required (CLO-approved text)
  - Brazil/CVM: 3-warning disclaimer required
  - US/FTC: Standards-compliant risk disclosure
  - AI disclosure required on all disclaimers (CLO-approved)
  - Hypothetical performance framing at Gate 4
  - "Expanding worldwide" claim flagged for CLO review
- **What is still unknown and must be treated as assumption:**
  - Smart contract audit decision and provider
  - Contract upgradeability decision
  - Exact on-ramp speed (target: same-day SEPA Instant)
  - Exact launch date within Q2 2026

## Multi-locale notes

- **Languages supported:** EN, DE, ES, PT-BR
- **Cultural adaptation rules:** Not translation — genuine cultural localization. Each locale requires its own voice, idioms, and cultural references. PT-BR must emphasize international transfers (Brazil already has PIX for domestic). DE must use consistent Sie/du.
- **Text expansion expectations:** German text ~30% longer than English. Spanish slightly longer. PT-BR comparable to English.
- **Locale-specific content differences:**
  - EN: Lead with yield (US savings 0.39% APY). De-emphasize P2P (Zelle is sufficient domestically).
  - DE: Lead with multi-asset access. Emphasize MiCA non-custodial advantage. Compare against Trade Republic + Revolut.
  - ES: Lead with accessibility and low minimums. Paga Extra (June + December) as cultural anchor.
  - PT-BR: Do NOT compete with PIX. Lead with "save in digital dollars — protect against BRL depreciation." Cross-border for 1.9M Brazilian diaspora in the US.
- **Regulatory text per locale:**
  - EN: FTC-compliant risk disclosure
  - DE: MiCA verbatim disclaimer (CLO-approved)
  - ES: MiCA verbatim disclaimer (CLO-approved)
  - PT-BR: CVM 3-warning disclaimer

## Handoff and scaling

- **Who else will use this brand system?** Solo founder (Bar) currently. Future team members, contractors.
- **Interactive tools to build:** Adelaide newsletter visual generator. Strategy comparison tool. Fee calculator.
- **What the AI should be able to generate on its own within brand constraints:** Marketing content, social media posts, newsletter sections, landing page sections, component variations — all within the Adelaide Filter.
- **What requires human review before publishing:** Any content claiming specific returns, any regulatory text changes, any content mentioning product capabilities not in the confirmed list, any content that could be interpreted as financial advice.
